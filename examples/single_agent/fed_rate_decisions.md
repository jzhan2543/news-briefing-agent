# Federal Reserve Holds Rates Steady at 3.5%-3.75% in March 2026, Projects Single Cut for Year Amid Geopolitical Uncertainty

## Executive Summary
The Federal Reserve held its benchmark interest rate steady at 3.5%-3.75% in March 2026, with officials projecting only one rate cut for the remainder of the year as they navigate higher-than-expected inflation and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East [source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/fed-interest-rate-decision-march-2026.html]. The decision, which passed with an 11-1 vote, reflects the central bank's cautious approach as it balances stronger economic growth projections against persistent inflationary pressures and uncertainty from an ongoing conflict with Iran [source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/fed-interest-rate-decision-march-2026.html]. Market analysts anticipate the Fed will maintain this holding pattern through the rest of 2026, with the next potential rate adjustment not expected until 2027 [source: https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/economy/fed-rate-cuts].

## Current Rate Environment and March Decision

The Federal Reserve voted 11-1 to maintain the federal funds rate in a range between 3.5%-3.75% at its March 18, 2026 meeting [source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/fed-interest-rate-decision-march-2026.html]. This decision to hold rates steady came following a series of rate cuts in late 2025 [source: https://equalsmoney.com/economic-calendar/events/fed-interest-rate-decision]. The March decision was widely anticipated by markets and represented a continuation of the Fed's current monetary policy stance [source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/fed-interest-rate-decision-march-2026.html].

The federal funds rate target range currently stands at 3.50% to 3.75%, with the next scheduled Federal Reserve interest rate decision set for Wednesday, April 29, 2026, at 18:00 GMT [source: https://equalsmoney.com/economic-calendar/events/fed-interest-rate-decision]. The Federal Reserve announces interest rate decisions eight times per year during Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meetings [source: https://equalsmoney.com/economic-calendar/events/fed-interest-rate-decision].
