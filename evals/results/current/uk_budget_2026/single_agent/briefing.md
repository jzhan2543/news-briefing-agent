# UK Autumn Budget 2026: Phased Tax Increases, Defence Spending Growth, and Mixed Business Response

## Executive Summary

The UK Autumn Budget 2025, delivered by Chancellor Rachel Reeves on November 26, 2025, introduces significant tax increases taking effect in 2026 and 2027, alongside substantial shifts in government spending priorities [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments]. The budget implements phased tax rate increases on dividend, property, and savings income, reduces certain investment tax reliefs, and introduces new VAT collection measures, while committing to major defence spending growth that constrains other departmental budgets [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments] [source: https://ifs.org.uk/sites/default/files/2025-09/UK_Defence_Spending_IFS_Green-Budget_2025_Chapter_0.pdf]. Business leaders have responded negatively to the measures, particularly regarding national insurance and minimum wage increases, though the budget does include some investment incentives for manufacturing [source: https://www.iod.com/news/uk-economy/iod-press-release-four-in-five-business-leaders-react-negatively-to-budget/] [source: https://www.beachmarketing.co.uk/uk-autumn-budget-impact-on-manufacturing-businesses/].

## Personal Income Tax Changes

The budget introduces a phased approach to increasing tax rates on different types of income. From April 2026, dividend income tax rates will increase by 2% across all bands: to 10.75% for basic rate taxpayers, 35.75% for higher rate taxpayers, and 39.35% for additional rate taxpayers [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments]. The dividend allowance will remain at £500, keeping the first £500 of dividend income tax-free [source: https://www.which.co.uk/news/article/11-tax-changes-to-know-about-in-2026-aNHEV0G8SSWI].

A second wave of increases follows in April 2027, when tax rates on property and savings income will rise by 2% to 22% for basic rate taxpayers, 42% for higher rate taxpayers, and 47% for additional rate taxpayers [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments].

Additionally, income tax and National Insurance thresholds will be frozen at current levels until April 2031, creating fiscal drag effects as more taxpayers are pulled into higher tax brackets through inflation [source: https://www.iser.essex.ac.uk/research/publications/working-papers/cempa/cempa3-26].

## Capital Gains Tax and Investment Incentives

The budget modifies several investment-related tax provisions. Capital Gains Tax rates for Business Asset Disposal Relief and Investors' Relief will increase from 14% to 18% [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx]. 

The income tax relief rate on Venture Capital Trust (VCT) investments will decrease from 30% to 20%, reducing the tax incentive for these investments [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx]. However, more medium-sized companies will become eligible to offer Enterprise Management Incentive (EMI) options to employees, expanding access to this employee share scheme [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx].

## VAT and Indirect Tax Measures

From an indirect tax perspective, the Autumn Budget contained no major surprises [source: https://johnstoncarmichael.com/insights/the-key-vat-announcements-from-the-autumn-budget]. The budget introduces new real-time VAT collection obligations for certain organizations, estimated to increase VAT revenue by nearly £700 million by 2029-30 [source: https://johnstoncarmichael.com/insights/the-key-vat-announcements-from-the-autumn-budget].

The removal of VAT relief for top-up payments on more expensive Motability lease vehicles will raise a further £300 million [source: https://johnstoncarmichael.com/insights/the-key-vat-announcements-from-the-autumn-budget]. Looking ahead, e-invoicing for VAT purposes will become mandatory for all business-to-business and business-to-government transactions from 2029, with a transition roadmap published at Budget 2026 [source: https://johnstoncarmichael.com/insights/the-key-vat-announcements-from-the-autumn-budget].

## Tax Administration and Digital Reporting

Making Tax Digital for income tax will become mandatory for individuals with business receipts exceeding £50,000, expanding digital tax reporting requirements [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx].

## Revenue Impact and Distributional Effects

The combined tax changes are projected to generate significant additional revenue. Tax revenue increases by approximately £1.4 billion in 2026, primarily driven by personal income tax changes that generate an additional £1.2 billion through threshold freezes [source: https://www.iser.essex.ac.uk/research/publications/working-papers/cempa/cempa3-26].

The distributional analysis shows modest poverty reduction effects. Overall poverty in the UK decreases by 21,738 people in 2026 after housing costs, with the poverty rate declining from 18.40% to 18.37% [source: https://www.iser.essex.ac.uk/research/publications/working-papers/cempa/cempa3-26]. This reduction is partly attributable to the removal of the Universal Credit two-child limit, scheduled to take effect from April 2026 [source: https://www.iser.essex.ac.uk/research/publications/working-papers/cempa/cempa3-26].

## Government Spending Priorities

The budget reflects a significant shift toward defence spending. The UK government has committed to spending 3.5% of national income on defence by 2035, with defence spending planned to increase in real terms by 2.7% between 2025-26 and 2026-27 [source: https://ifs.org.uk/sites/default/files/2025-09/UK_Defence_Spending_IFS_Green-Budget_2025_Chapter_0.pdf].

This defence spending commitment creates fiscal pressures elsewhere. Other departmental spending in England is planned to be almost flat between 2026-27 and 2027-28, reflecting rapid growth in planned defence spending during those years [source: https://ifs.org.uk/sites/default/files/2025-09/UK_Defence_Spending_IFS_Green-Budget_2025_Chapter_0.pdf]. Analysis warns that rapidly increasing planned spending on defence carries the risk that additional money will not be spent well or at all [source: https://ifs.org.uk/sites/default/files/2025-09/UK_Defence_Spending_IFS_Green-Budget_2025_Chapter_0.pdf].

Healthcare infrastructure does see increased investment, with the 2025 Autumn Budget confirming that the Department of Health and Social Care's capital budgets will rise to £15.2 billion by the end of the period [source: https://questions-statements.parliament.uk/written-questions/detail/2025-12-04/97099/].

The UK government's budgeted public sector expenditure for 2026/27 is broken down by function including defence, public order and safety, environment protection, education, social protection, and economic affairs [source: https://www.statista.com/statistics/298524/government-spending-in-the-uk/?srsltid=AfmBOorYv4omXsqqwNGocqrsvRM9Aaa6Z6E3iw0EPErH71SLrC0TJcDa].

## Business and Sector-Specific Impacts

The budget has generated overwhelmingly negative reactions from the business community. Four in five business leaders reacted negatively to the Budget according to the Institute of Directors [source: https://www.iod.com/news/uk-economy/iod-press-release-four-in-five-business-leaders-react-negatively-to-budget/]. Business leaders cited increases in national insurance as a key concern, with the minimum wage increase identified as another major point of criticism [source: https://www.iod.com/news/uk-economy/iod-press-release-four-in-five-business-leaders-react-negatively-to-budget/].

For manufacturing businesses specifically, the budget presents a mixed picture. The UK Autumn Budget 2026 introduces investment incentives for manufacturing businesses while simultaneously creating tighter profit margins [source: https://www.beachmarketing.co.uk/uk-autumn-budget-impact-on-manufacturing-businesses/]. Marketing becomes increasingly important for manufacturing businesses in response to the budget changes [source: https://www.beachmarketing.co.uk/uk-autumn-budget-impact-on-manufacturing-businesses/].
