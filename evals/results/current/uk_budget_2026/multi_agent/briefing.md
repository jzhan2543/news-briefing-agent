# UK Autumn Budget 2026: Tax Rate Increases on Dividends, Property, and Savings Income

**Executive Summary:** Chancellor Rachel Reeves delivered the UK Autumn Budget on 26 November 2025, introducing significant tax increases taking effect in 2026 and 2027. Dividend tax rates will rise by 2 percentage points from April 2026, while property and savings income tax rates will increase by 2 percentage points from April 2027. [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments] The government also extended income tax threshold freezes until April 2031, a measure expected to raise £23 billion through fiscal drag. [source: https://www.sjp.co.uk/individuals/news/autumn-budget-sweeping-up-the-budget-announcements]

## Dividend Tax Changes

From April 2026, dividend tax rates will increase by 2 percentage points across basic and higher rate bands. [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments] The new rates will be 10.75% for basic rate taxpayers and 35.75% for higher rate taxpayers, while the additional rate remains unchanged at 39.35%. [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments] The dividend allowance will remain frozen at £500, meaning only the first £500 of dividend income stays tax-free. [source: https://www.which.co.uk/news/article/11-tax-changes-to-know-about-in-2026-aNHEV0G8SSWI]

## Property and Savings Income Tax Increases

Starting in April 2027, tax rates on property and savings income will rise by 2 percentage points. [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments] The new rates will be 22% for basic rate taxpayers, 42% for higher rate taxpayers, and 47% for additional rate taxpayers. [source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/united-kingdom/individual/significant-developments]

## Income Tax Threshold Freezes and Fiscal Drag

The government extended the freeze on income tax thresholds for an additional three years until April 2031. [source: https://www.sjp.co.uk/individuals/news/autumn-budget-sweeping-up-the-budget-announcements] This measure is projected to raise £23 billion over the period through fiscal drag, as more taxpayers are pulled into higher tax brackets due to wage inflation while thresholds remain static. [source: https://www.sjp.co.uk/individuals/news/autumn-budget-sweeping-up-the-budget-announcements] Analysis indicates tax revenue will increase by approximately £1.4 billion in 2026, with £1.2 billion coming from personal income tax changes driven by these threshold freezes. [source: https://www.iser.essex.ac.uk/research/publications/working-papers/cempa/cempa3-26]

## Capital Gains Tax and Investment Incentives

Capital gains tax rates where Business Asset Disposal Relief or Investors' Relief applies will increase from 14% to 18%. [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx] The income tax relief available on Venture Capital Trust (VCT) investments will be reduced from 30% to 20% from April 2026. [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx] [source: https://www.sjp.co.uk/individuals/news/autumn-budget-sweeping-up-the-budget-announcements]

## VAT and Compliance Changes

The Budget introduced new VAT-collecting obligations for certain organizations, estimated to increase VAT revenue by nearly £700 million by 2029-30. [source: https://johnstoncarmichael.com/insights/the-key-vat-announcements-from-the-autumn-budget] The removal of VAT relief for top-up payments on more expensive vehicles leased through Motability is projected to raise an additional £300 million by the same timeframe. [source: https://johnstoncarmichael.com/insights/the-key-vat-announcements-from-the-autumn-budget]

## Business Tax Administration

Making Tax Digital (MTD) for income tax will be mandated for individuals with over £50,000 in business receipts. [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx] The government also announced changes to expand eligibility for Enterprise Management Incentive (EMI) options, allowing more medium-sized companies to offer these employee share schemes. [source: https://taxscape.deloitte.com/article/uk-tax-landscape--key-changes-for-2026.aspx]
